#            ** OPEN AI INVESTIGATION REPORT**

        
OpenAI is the company behind ChatGPT. It started in 2015 as a nonprofit AI research lab and has since grown into one of the most valuable private tech companies in the world. 

* **CEO:** Sam Altman  
* **President:** Greg Brockman  
* Other top execs handle finance, strategy, and revenue (CFO, CSO, CRO). A few top leaders have recently stepped back temporarily for health reasons, causing some reshuffling of duties.

**STRUTURE**

* OpenAI has two parts: a nonprofit (OpenAI Foundation) that controls a for-profit company (OpenAI Group PBC).  
* Microsoft owns a large chunk (\~27%) and gets a cut (20%) of OpenAI's revenue through 2032\.  
* OpenAI has filed paperwork to eventually go public (IPO), though there's no confirmed date yet.


**WORTH**

* Valuation: around $852 billion (as of a funding round closed in March 2026).  
* Revenue: roughly $20 billion a year, mostly from ChatGPT subscriptions and business API use.  
* Losses: still losing a lot of money (\~$14 billion expected in 2026\) because running AI models is expensive. Not expected to turn a profit until around 2029\.


**PRODUCTS**

* ChatGPT — the main product, used by hundreds of millions of people weekly.  
* API — lets other companies build tools using OpenAI's AI.  
* Codex — a coding assistant tool.  
* DALL·E / Whisper — image generation and speech-to-text tools.  
* Sora (video generator) was shut down in March 2026 — it cost far more to run than it earned.


**PARTNERS**

* Microsoft — biggest partner and investor, powers Azure/Copilot integration.  
* Nvidia, Oracle, SoftBank, Amazon — supply computing power or have invested billions.  
* Disney — had a deal to use Disney characters in Sora, but it fell apart when Sora shut down.


**LEGAL TROUBLE**

* Elon Musk lawsuit: Musk sued OpenAI over its shift away from nonprofit status. A jury sided with OpenAI in May 2026\.  
* Copyright lawsuits**:** Authors, publishers, and The New York Times are suing OpenAI over using their content to train AI. Still ongoing.  
* Harm-related lawsuits: More than 20 lawsuits claim ChatGPT contributed to real-world harm (including a Florida state lawsuit and several wrongful-death cases). OpenAI says it's improving safety features in response.

OpenAI is growing fast, spending even faster, heading toward a possible IPO, and facing serious legal challenges on multiple fronts — copyright, safety, and its own founding mission.
